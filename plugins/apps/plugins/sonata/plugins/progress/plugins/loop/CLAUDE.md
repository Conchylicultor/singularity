# loop

Sonata A–B practice loop: a draggable loop region on the progression bar, a
toolbar Loop toggle, and `L`/`[`/`]` shortcuts that cycle playback within `[A, B]`.

The loop is also surfaced on the piano roll's falling-note timeline — a
scroll-synced `[A, B]` band with `A`/`B` boundary lines, contributed (view-only)
through the generic `Sonata.TransportOverlay` slot so it scrolls glued to the
notes. Dragging the bounds stays on the progression bar.

Because the loop is usually taller than the lookahead, a boundary often sits
off-screen. The loop also surfaces a sticky off-screen `A`/`B` edge indicator —
a small arrow chip pinned to the lane's top/bottom edge (screen-anchored via the
new `Sonata.TransportEdge` slot, mounted outside the scroll layer) pointing
toward a boundary that has scrolled past the lookahead, complementing the
scroll-synced band. The content-space band label and the edge chip are mutually
exclusive: a single edge-bucket threshold (which also reserves the HUD's vertical
zone) decides per boundary whether its in-band label or its edge chip shows.

The loop transport state (`loop`/`setLoop`) and the rAF wrap live in the `shell`
plugin alongside `isPlaying`/`tempoScale`; this plugin owns the whole UI.

**v1 caveat — audio gap at wrap:** the wrap bumps `seekEpoch`, which reschedules
the audio engine from `loop.start`. That restart leaves a small audible gap at
each loop boundary (the visual cursor jumps seamlessly). Accepted for v1.
