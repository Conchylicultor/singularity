# chord-progression

The progression strip beside the piano roll: every chord of the song as a chip,
laid out bar-by-bar with each chip sized by its duration, the chord under the
playhead highlighted, and click-to-seek.

## Rhythm model (the `(C .. D)` mini-language, visualized)

The strip never re-parses the chord-grid mini-language. It reads the canonical
`ChordAnnotation`s from the `Score` (so authored chord-grids *and* analyzer-
derived chords render identically) and slices them against the score's bar grid
(`bars()` from `score/core`). Within each bar, a chord occupies a `fr`-weighted
slot equal to its beat-span in that bar (inline-grid `gridTemplateColumns`), which
reconstructs the language's rhythm directly from the beats:

- a whole-bar chord → one full-width chip;
- a group `(E E6)` → two half-width chips sharing the bar;
- an in-bar hold `(C . . D)` → C at ¾ width, D at ¼;
- a cross-bar hold `Cmaj7 . .` → a struck chip, then dimmed *continuation* chips
  carried into the following bars (a visual tie).

Bars are arranged `BARS_PER_ROW` (4) across; head/tail rest bars are trimmed so
the strip starts and ends on a chord. The active chord is tracked with
`useCursorSelector` (reconciles only on chord boundaries, not per frame) and
matched to chips by reference equality against the memoized chord array.
