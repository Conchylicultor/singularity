# metronome

Sonata metronome: a synthesized click track plus an optional count-in lead-in.

Clicks are scheduled on the audio engine's live `AudioContext` (via
`useAudioGraph()`) — never a separate context — so they align sample-accurately
to playback, and they route to `ctx.destination` directly (independent of the
music master gain) so muting the song keeps the click. The **continuous** track
is a synthetic click-note list (one note per beat, downbeat accented) fed through
the engine's own `startScheduling`, inheriting seamless A–B loop wrap and
tempo-retime for free. The **count-in** schedules its lead-in clicks against the
audio clock and ends them with a single `ConstantSourceNode.onended` (audio-clock
driven, no JS timer) that calls the transport's `finishCountIn()` to begin real
playback from the parked start beat; an on-screen countdown HUD reads the same
clock. Settings (continuous on/off, count-in bars, click volume, accent
downbeat) live in the `sonata.metronome` config.
