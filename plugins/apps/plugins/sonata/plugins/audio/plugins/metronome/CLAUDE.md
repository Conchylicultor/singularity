# metronome

Sonata metronome: a synthesized click track plus an optional count-in lead-in.

Clicks are scheduled on the audio engine's live `AudioContext` (via
`useAudioGraph()`) — never a separate context — so they align sample-accurately
to playback, and they route to `ctx.destination` directly (independent of the
music master gain) so muting the song keeps the click. The **continuous** track
is a synthetic click-note list fed through the engine's own `startScheduling`,
inheriting seamless A–B loop wrap and tempo-retime for free. It carries three
accent tiers via a `pitch` sentinel — downbeat (accented), main beat, and, when
the **subdivision** setting is >1, a lighter/quieter tick on each in-between
subdivision cell (`beatGrid(score, subdivision)`, so 2 = eighths, 3 = triplets,
4 = sixteenths). The subdivision only *inserts* finer clicks between the notated
beats; the song's own meter (beats-per-bar, downbeats) is always the source of
truth and is never overridden. The **count-in** schedules its lead-in clicks
against the audio clock (one click per beat, no subdivision) and ends them with a
single `ConstantSourceNode.onended` (audio-clock driven, no JS timer) that calls
the transport's `finishCountIn()` to begin real playback from the parked start
beat; an on-screen countdown HUD reads the same clock. Settings (continuous
on/off, subdivision, count-in bars, click volume, accent downbeat) live in the
`sonata.metronome` config.
