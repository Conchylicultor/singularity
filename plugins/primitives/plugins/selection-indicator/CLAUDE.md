# selection-indicator

Presentational checkbox / radio **indicator** boxes — the small filled/outline
square or circle next to a selectable option. `CheckboxIndicator` and
`RadioIndicator` take a single `checked` boolean (plus an optional `className`
for layout, e.g. `mt-0.5`).

The point of this primitive is that the **fixed shape lives in one place**: a
checkbox is a `rounded-checkbox` square, a radio is a `rounded-full` circle, and
neither reads `--radius`, so both keep their semantic shape under every Shape
preset. Reach for these instead of hand-rolling a styled box with a radius class
— that's how the AskUserQuestion checkbox once rounded into a near-circle under a
generous preset and looked like a radio.

Scope: these are **presentational only** (they render the visual, not an
interactive control). Native `<input type="checkbox">` form controls — e.g.
`multi-select`'s `SelectionCheckbox`, the page to-do block — are UA-drawn and out
of scope; they have no `--radius` rounding problem. See
[`radius/CLAUDE.md`](../radius/CLAUDE.md) for the `rounded-checkbox` fixed shape.
